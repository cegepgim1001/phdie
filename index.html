A Framework for Sustainable and Secure Smart Shelters

This portfolio outlines a PhD research project at the intersection of robotics, blockchain, and AI. The goal is to develop a novel Cyber-Physical System (CPS) for clustered, "Earthship-inspired" sustainable shelters, focusing on automated resource management, decentralized governance, and robust cybersecurity.

Explore the Project

Core Technology Pillars

The project integrates three advanced technology domains to create a resilient and autonomous living environment.

Robotics and Automation for Sustainable Habitats

The project's foundation is a robotic and IoT layer for autonomous operation. Leveraging a Robot Operating System (ROS), this component will manage automated tasks critical for self-sufficiency. This includes designing and prototyping systems for monitoring hydroponic/aquaponic gardens, optimizing solar panel alignment for maximum energy capture, and regulating thermal pumps for efficient climate control. The architecture will support decentralized, real-time decision-making, allowing each shelter to operate independently while collaborating with the larger community cluster.

Blockchain and Smart Contracts for Decentralized Governance

To ensure fair and transparent community governance, this project integrates a blockchain framework. Smart contracts will automate agreements for shared resources, such as the distribution of collected water or surplus energy, with all transactions recorded on an immutable ledger. To protect residents' privacy, Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKPs) will be explored to verify resource usage and compliance with community rules without revealing sensitive data, striking a crucial balance between accountability and personal privacy.

AI & Cybersecurity for System Hardening

With deep expertise in cybersecurity, this component focuses on protecting the entire system. A comprehensive threat model will address vulnerabilities across the physical, IoT, network, and blockchain layers. AI-powered tools will be implemented for real-time threat detection and anomaly analysis. The research will involve designing and implementing robust security protocols to protect all communications between devices, smart contracts, and user interfaces, ensuring the shelter community's autonomy and integrity remain uncompromised.

3-Year Research Roadmap

A structured plan for the doctoral research, from foundational work to final thesis.

Year 1: Foundational Research & Prototyping

Focus on literature review, defining the CPS architecture, and developing initial prototypes for the robotics and IoT layer using ROS. Begin development of core smart contracts for resource management.

Year 2: Integration & Security Implementation

Integrate the blockchain governance model with the IoT/robotics framework. Develop and implement the comprehensive cybersecurity threat model and deploy AI-powered monitoring tools. Conduct initial simulation testing.

Year 3: System Validation & Thesis Completion

Conduct large-scale simulations and potentially physical test-bed validation of the integrated system. Analyze results, refine the framework, and focus on writing the PhD thesis and publishing findings in top-tier journals.
